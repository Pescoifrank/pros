Computer Networks Explained Simply

Understanding how data moves—from routers to firewalls—is key in today's connected world. Here's how it works.

1. What is a Computer Network?

A computer network is a group of computers and devices connected together to share resources like files, internet, and printers.

2. How Data Moves in a Network

When you send a message or open a website, your data is split into packets. These packets travel through cables or wireless signals, passing through multiple devices to reach their destination.

3. Routers and Switches

Routers direct data between networks (like your home and the internet), while switches manage traffic inside a single network like a company office.

4. IP Addresses and DNS

Every device has a unique IP address. DNS (Domain Name System) translates human-readable names (like google.com) into IP addresses so computers can communicate.

5. Firewalls and Security

Firewalls act as gatekeepers, monitoring traffic in and out of the network and blocking malicious data. They can be software or hardware-based.

6. Wired vs Wireless Networks

Wired networks use cables for speed and stability, while wireless networks (Wi-Fi) provide mobility but may be affected by distance and interference.

7. Network Protocols

Protocols like TCP/IP, HTTP, FTP, and HTTPS define the rules for data communication and ensure devices can understand each other.

8. The Internet vs Intranet

The internet is a global network. An intranet is a private network used inside companies for internal communication and resource sharing.
